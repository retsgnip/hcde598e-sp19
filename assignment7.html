Assignment 7: Final project proposal

Proposal

For the final project, I decided to create a decorative wall-mounted moose head wall sculpture. These heads are always impressive when they are in a cabin. I’ll also add a light so that it lights up in the dark. Part of the design will incorporate a way to easily access the light.

 

Timeline and tasks

The schedule will be tight and will require a quick turnaround. I’m budgeting a day for contingencies to handle any unexpected issues that may arise during the design and assembly of this sculpture.

 

Date Task
May 29 to May 30
  • Design the shape of the moose head in Rhino3D in both lateral and anterior views

  • From a lateral view, splice the shape of the head into sections. These sections will then be extruded programmatically in Grasshopper. Slices to the anterior will be smaller as they form the mouth and snout. Slices to the posterior will be bigger as they form the eyes and the back of the head.

  • Include a cavity to hold a light. Include an opening at the top of the structure for easy access to the light.

  • Design a skeleton with slots where the above slices will fit into.
  • Design a mounting board

 
May 31 to June 2
  • 3D print the skeleton pieces as they need to bend in all 3 axis going from the back of the head to the snout
  • Laser cut acetal copolymer cross-section slices
  • Laser cut the mounting board

 
June 3
  • Assembly of the pieces

 
June 4
  • Contingency and buffer day

 

 

Materials

  • 2x Acetal copolymer plastic sheets
  • 1 spindle PLA filament
  • 1x large cardboard for initial fitting purposes
